MRS RITA ESPINA-Riddle, CESO V, Schools

Division Office-Makati City superintendent, ap- pointed three officials effective March 27, 2019.

Dr Rosalie Asoy-Trongco was appointed as

the new School Governance and Operations

Division chief education supervisor.

Dr Trongco, former public schools district

supervisor, replaced Mr Claudio Palado Rojo

who retired in August 2018.

Meanwhile, Mr Michael R Lee, former

public schools district supervisor and division

mathematics coordinator, and Mr Neil Vincent

Cedilla Sandoval, Learning Resource Manage- ment and Development System (LRMDS)

coordinator, were appointed as the mathematics

education program supervisor and the LRMDS

education program supervisor, respectively.

Mr Lee replaced Mrs Angelita Sagun

Jalimao who was promoted as the Curriculum

Implementation Division chief education supervi- sor, while Mr Sandoval replaced Mrs Imelda

Caravaca-Ferrer who was named as the public

schools district supervisor.

The announcement for the newly-appointed

SDO-Makati officials was made through a

DepEd-Makati Unnumbered Memorandum by

Mrs Riddle on April 1, 2019.

FROM HEAD TO toe.

A total of 90 193 public school pupils,

students, and learners from 25 public elemen- tary and 12 public secondary schools in the

Schools Division of Makati City benefitted from

the Expanded Project FREE (Free, Relevant, and

Excellent Education) of the City Government of

Makati and the City of Makati School Board

through its Special Education Fund.

The city government allocated around PhP700

million for the Expanded Project FREE to provide

public school pupils, students, and learners with

free bags, school supplies, textbooks and work- books, school and physical education uniforms, and

information and communication technology tools.

Free school bags were also given to Grade

1 pupils, kindergarten pupils, and Special

Education (SpEd) learners.

Aside from providing with free school

supplies and school uniforms, public school

pupils, students, and learners also received

raincoats, dental kits, rain boots, anti-dengue

kits, and emergency go bags.

“Expanded Project FREE is really helpful

especially to those students who do not have

the capacity to buy school supplies that they

need in school. These projects help a lot of

learners in Makati to study because the city

government provides our school supplies so we

will be motivated to go to school every day,”

said Junia Melone Abrinica Barsalote, a sixth

grade pupil of the Rizal Elementary School.

More recently, the city government

distributed rubber shoes to public school pupils,

students, and learners.

BY JAYSON ORDINARIO CARAANG

REALIZING THE VITAL role that teachers play

in nation buiding, the Schools Division Office- Makati City, under the leadership of Mrs Rita

Espina- Riddle, CESO V, schools division super- intendent, recognized 594 public elementary

and secondary school teachers during the

DepEd-Makati Second Gawad Katapatan at

the Benigno “Ninoy” Aquino High School,

Barangay Comembo, City of Makati,

December 18, 2019.

Teacher awardees with 10 and 15 years

of service received bronze pins; teachers who

served 20, 25, and 30 years were given silver

rings; teachers with 35 and 40 years were

awarded gold medallions; while retired school

principals and division officials were given

14-karat gold bracelets.

Gawad Katapatan is part of the PRAISE

(Program on Awards and Incentives for Service

Excellence) Program of the SDO-Makati that

recognizes teachers who serve Makati public

elementary and secondary schools.
